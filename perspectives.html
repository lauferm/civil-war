Perspectives and Thoughts on the American Civil War

The American Civil War was a time of intense political, social, and ideological division. Both the Union and Confederacy had their own perspectives and thoughts on the war and what it meant for the future of the country. Many in the North saw the war as a struggle to preserve the Union and end slavery, while many in the South saw it as a defense of their rights and way of life. The war also sparked debates about states' rights, the role of the federal government, and the nature of the Constitution. In the aftermath of the war, the country worked to rebuild and come to terms with the changes that had occurred.
