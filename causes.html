Causes of the American Civil War

The American Civil War was fought between 1861 and 1865 and was one of the most significant events in American history. The war was a result of a long-standing conflict between the North and the South over a variety of issues, including slavery, states' rights, and economic differences. The election of Abraham Lincoln in 1860 was a major catalyst for the start of the war. Lincoln was a strong opponent of slavery and many in the South feared that he would try to abolish it. This led several Southern states to secede from the Union and form the Confederate States of America.
